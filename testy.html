DOCUMENTATION

HTML/CSS Validation

My HTML pages validated. However, it is to be noted that there is a general warning that the validator flagged:

Warning: Section lacks heading. Consider using h2-h6 elements to add identifying headings to all sections.
which is actually a decision I made due to aesthetic reasonings. I don't want to have headers everywhere in my page but I still wanted to divide my page up into sections.

The validator complained about my moz-box-shadow and moz-border-radius, as well as the webkit-box-radius and webkit-box-shadow commands, stating that they're not supported.

Speed of Pages

The site took about 1.5seconds to load. The most image intensive page which contains my portfolio took 2.2seconds to load. As I had used images that I had first edited on Photoshop instead of loading full resolution images, the site didn't take too long to load.

Flexibility

There is a degree of flexibility and responsiveness of my site. This is due to the fact that I used the floating layout which usually works well enough for mobile devices. The maximum width of my site is also placed at a comfortable 800px which will fit most screens well enough.

Accessibility

According to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines, I probably need to adjust some of the font sizes. At the moment, most of my fonts are at an automatic weight and size, but as I am using some Google Fonts, that could mean that some of the fonts may be too small for some users. That said, I did provide alternative font types along with the ones that I have specified to overcome that issue.

Browser Independence

I tested the site on Google Chrome, Safari, and Firefox. In all the different browsers, the site works the way I had expected. In Firefox, the colors are a little more muted. When the Java Script was turned off, the site still works just fine as the only thing that was really affected was the slideshow.
